John Winston Ono Lennon, the visionary behind The Beatles and one of the most influential musicians of all time, has died at the age of 85. He passed away peacefully in his home in New York City late last night, surrounded by family and close friends.

🕊️

"Imagine all the people living life in peace. John lived that dream until the very end. The world is darker today."

— Yoko Ono Lennon, in a statement released this morning

The news was confirmed early this morning by his family. A spokesperson said Lennon had been in declining health for several months but remained active, working on a final collection of unreleased demos and letters he planned to publish later this year.

Born October 9, 1940 in Liverpool, England, Lennon rose to global fame as a founding member of The Beatles. After the band’s breakup in 1970, he continued to create boundary-pushing solo work including the timeless album Imagine. He was a fierce advocate for peace, women’s rights, and against war throughout his long life.

Tributes have already begun pouring in from musicians, world leaders, and fans around the globe. Paul McCartney posted simply: “My brother. Rest in peace, John.” Rolling Stone called him “the voice that defined a generation — and then kept redefining it for six decades.”

Funeral arrangements are expected to be announced later today. A public memorial service will be held at Strawberry Fields in Central Park, the site where fans have gathered for decades.
